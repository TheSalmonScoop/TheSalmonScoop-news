NO MORE SALMON FISHES

Umpqua River anglers report historic shortage of fish in Reedsport; officials unable to locate any salmon willing to comment.
Front-page photo placeholder: Salmon Harbor Marina, empty cooler, foggy dock, and one extremely patient fishing rod
A cooler waits near the dock while anglers compare theories, snacks, and increasingly complicated excuses.
By Randy Hookwell · River Desk Correspondent · Friday, July 17, 2026

REEDSPORT, Ore., July 17, 2026 — Panic rippled through boat ramps and bait counters this week after anglers on the lower Umpqua River reported they were not catching salmon fishes no more.

The situation became serious when several fishermen returned to the dock with empty coolers, damp sandwiches, and explanations that grew more elaborate with every retelling.

“I have fished this river my whole life,” said Gary Thompson, who declined to reveal his secret location because it still was not working. “Yesterday I caught a stick, a leaf, and what I believe was my own confidence leaving the boat.”

Local theories include the salmon relocating to Alaska, hiding in an underwater homeowners association, or refusing to participate until anglers stop taking grip-and-grin photos.

Bait shops reported a sharp increase in coffee, jerky, and “maybe tomorrow” purchases. One customer bought a new net, then admitted the net was mostly for emotional support.
