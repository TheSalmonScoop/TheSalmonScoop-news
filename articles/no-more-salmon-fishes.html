NO MORE SALMON FISHES

Umpqua River anglers report historic shortage of fish in Reedsport.
By Randy Hookwell · River Desk Correspondent · Friday, July 17, 2026
Feature photo placeholder
A realistic newspaper-style image slot for the story.

REEDSPORT, Ore., July 17, 2026 — Panic rippled through boat ramps and bait counters this week after anglers on the lower Umpqua River reported they were not catching salmon fishes no more.

Several fishermen returned to the dock with empty coolers, damp sandwiches, and explanations that grew more elaborate with every retelling.

“Yesterday I caught a stick, a leaf, and what I believe was my own confidence leaving the boat.”

Local theories include the salmon relocating to Alaska, hiding in an underwater homeowners association, or refusing to participate until anglers stop taking grip-and-grin photos.

A spokesperson for the river could not be reached. The tide, however, continued coming in and going out like it knew something everyone else did not.

Bait shops reported a sharp increase in coffee, jerky, and “maybe tomorrow” purchases. One customer bought a new net, then admitted the net was mostly for emotional support.

At press time, an angler claimed he saw a salmon roll near the bridge. After review, witnesses agreed it may have been a seal, a log, or wishful thinking.
