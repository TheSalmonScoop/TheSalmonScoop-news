NO MORE SALMON FISHES

Umpqua River anglers report historic shortage of fish in Reedsport.
By Randy Hookwell, River Desk Correspondent
Fictional News Photo Placeholder
Image placeholder for satirical article layout.

REEDSPORT, Ore. — Panic rippled through boat ramps and bait counters this week after anglers on the lower Umpqua River reported they were not catching salmon fishes no more.

Several fishermen returned to the dock with empty coolers, damp sandwiches, and explanations that grew more elaborate with every retelling.

“I have fished this river my whole life,” said Gary Thompson. “Yesterday I caught a stick, a leaf, and what I believe was my own confidence leaving the boat.”

Local theories include the salmon relocating to Alaska, hiding in an underwater homeowners association, or refusing to participate until anglers stop taking grip-and-grin photos.

Bait shops reported a sharp increase in coffee, jerky, and “maybe tomorrow” purchases. One customer bought a new net, then admitted the net was mostly for emotional support.

At press time, one angler claimed to see a salmon roll near the bridge. After review, witnesses agreed the sighting may have been a seal, a log, or wishful thinking.
